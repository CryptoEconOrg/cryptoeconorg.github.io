ABOUT

CryptoEcon aims to foster the development of rigor in cryptoeconomic analysis.​ ​Scaling Bitcoin​ has become a center of excellence for the evaluation of technical research, including aspects of cryptography and distributed systems. While other conferences provide a mix of technical and economic content, the focus tends to be less rigorous. Economic discourse in general is often a mixture of predictions and other supposition. There is to date no center of excellence for cryptoeconomics.

Economics is a broad set of disciplines. Bitcoiners tend to align on ​Austrian Economics​, yet this is as well a fragmented discipline. Conference economic theory will be limited to ​Catallactics​ - a subdiscipline based on rational deduction from a set of axioms. The axioms are assumptions obtained from empirical observation, specifically ​Human Action​ and ​Time Preference​. Concepts will be built up from these first principles, incorporating unique characteristics of Bitcoin.

The sessions assume a working understanding of Bitcoin but little understanding of economic theory. Content will be presented in a logical progression, developing an understanding of rational economic theory with application to Bitcoin. The bulk of content will be provided in a seminar format led by Eric Voskuil, author of ​Cryptoeconomics​ and lead maintainer of Libbitcoin. Guest speakers and panelists will provide perspective on the state of the industry as it pertains to theory, including distributed exchanges, markets, hardware and mining.
